Bangladesh has significant potential to double or even triple its current production of vegetables to meet domestic demand and create a substantial surplus for export. At present, the country supplies only 2 to 3 percent of the international demand for vegetables. However, this market share has considerable potential to grow rapidly, provided proper plans are formulated and implemented sincerely. Cultivating vegetables for export can quickly become a highly rewarding activity if supported by appropriate policies. Notably, many educated, unemployed individuals are adopting vegetable farming as a form of self-employment, which is undoubtedly contributing to increased production.
