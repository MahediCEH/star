Can NFL games end in a tie after overtime?

In the fast-paced world of the NFL, where every play counts and every second matters, the idea of a game ending without a winner might sound surprising. However, under certain circumstances, NFL games can end in a tie — even after overtime. While ties are rare in professional football, they are an official part of the league’s rulebook.

If you've ever wondered how an NFL game can end without a winner, this article breaks down the rules, history, and what happens during overtime.

Can an NFL Game End in a Tie?

Yes, NFL games can end in a tie, but only during the regular season. If the score is still tied at the end of one overtime period, the game is officially declared a tie. However, this rule only applies to regular season games — postseason games cannot end in a tie, as they must continue with additional overtime periods until a winner is determined.

How Does NFL Overtime Work?

The NFL uses a unique overtime format designed to give both teams a fair chance to win the game. Here's how it works:

Regular Season Overtime Rules (2024 Season)
  • Overtime lasts for one 10-minute period.
  • A coin toss determines which team will receive the ball first.
  • If the team that receives the ball scores a touchdown on their first possession, the game ends immediately.
  • If the receiving team scores only a field goal, the opposing team gets one possession to either tie the game or win with a touchdown.
  • If neither team scores or if both teams score the same amount of points, the game ends in a tie after 10 minutes.

Postseason Overtime Rules

In the playoffs, the rules are slightly different to ensure there is always a winner:
  • The game continues with 15-minute overtime periods until one team scores more points.
  • Both teams are guaranteed at least one possession, even if the first team scores a touchdown.

How Common Are Ties in the NFL?

Ties are very rare in the NFL. On average, there are only a few ties per decade. Since the league shortened overtime from 15 minutes to 10 minutes in 2017, ties have become slightly more frequent.

Ties may frustrate fans and players alike, but they can have significant playoff implications when it comes to seeding and tiebreakers.

Why Does the NFL Allow Ties?

The NFL introduced the tie rule to prioritize player safety and avoid excessively long games. Unlike other sports like hockey or baseball, football is highly physical, and extended games could increase the risk of injuries.

By capping regular season overtime at one 10-minute period, the league balances competition with player well-being.

Do Ties Affect Playoff Standings?

Yes, ties can significantly impact playoff seeding. In the standings, a tie counts as half a win and half a loss — making a team's record look like 7-8-1 instead of 7-9 or 8-8. This unique scoring method can break ties between two teams fighting for a playoff spot.

While ties in the NFL may seem unusual, they are a part of the league's rules — serving both competitive fairness and player safety. In the regular season, games can end in a tie if neither team scores enough points during the 10-minute overtime period. However, once the playoffs begin, the NFL ensures every game ends with a winner — no matter how long it takes.

Love them or hate them, ties remind us that in the NFL, sometimes the fiercest battles still leave both teams standing.

The NFL expanded the regular season from 16 to 17 games in 2021, marking the first major schedule change since 1978. This change was implemented to provide more entertainment for fans and increase revenue opportunities.

Preseason Games

Playoff Games

  • Regular Season: 272 games

What Is Fantasy Football?

Fantasy football is a strategic game where participants act as team managers, selecting NFL players for their virtual rosters. Points are scored based on the real-life performance of these players, such as touchdowns, yards gained, or field goals scored. Participants compete in weekly matchups within their league, making lineup adjustments and trades to optimize their team's performance.

  3. Transparency and Integrity

The $250 cap might seem arbitrary, but it serves a critical purpose. Fantasy football leagues often include cash prizes, and without this limit, there could be concerns about NFL personnel using their insider knowledge for financial gain. By keeping the prize modest, the league ensures that the focus remains on fun and camaraderie rather than profit.

Fantasy football isn’t just a game for fans; it’s a significant driver of engagement for the NFL. Interestingly, this engagement also extends to NFL players themselves, who enjoy the unique opportunity to experience football from a fan’s perspective. For many players, participating in fantasy football is a way to connect with their teammates and loved ones while celebrating the game they love.

    While fantasy football is not classified as gambling, the overlap in structure—prizes, drafts, and competitive stakes—continues to spark debate. The NFL’s firm stance on financial limits helps to mitigate these concerns, but the conversation persists.

Conversely, running shoes are made specifically for the forward, repetitive motion of running. Their grooved soles provide better traction on a variety of surfaces, including concrete and grass. Running shoes come in different thicknesses to fit a variety of terrains and have a more flexible sole. This flexibility guarantees comfort and support customized to each runner's requirements.

Reasons Why Running in Basketball Shoes Is Not a Good Idea

2. Risk of Injuries: Because basketball shoes don't provide enough cushioning or support, wearing them while running raises your risk of developing plantar fasciitis, tendonitis, and shin splints.

3. Ankle Stability: In order to give ankle stability during lateral movements, basketball shoes are frequently high-cut, which makes them less appropriate for the prolonged, repetitive motion of running.

4. Weight: Running shoes are made to be lightweight and comfortable for prolonged running sessions, whereas basketball shoes are typically heavier.

In conclusion, it's not advised to run in basketball shoes, even though it's technically possible.
